Analysis Dashboard

Python / Streamlit / Pandas / Plotly / PostgreSQL

The analysis dashboard is a tool that I developed throughout Spring 2023 for OCF’s internal use and continues to evolve. Built with Streamlit, a python-based framework made specifically for creating data apps, the dashboard tracks and displays Quartz Solar’s mean absolute error (MAE) on both the national and GSP level. Our database generates this statistic using PVLive day-after updated values as the baseline for measuring error, the larger the error, the less accurate the forecast. Thanks to the analysis dashboard, OCF has a valuable feedback tool for understanding the accuracy of both the Quartz Solar forecast and other models in production.

about me

who i am


I am a gardener and a Full Stack Developer based between Lille, France and a village along the River Leie near Ghent, Belgium. I am American and French. I grew up outside of Chicago and have lived and worked in Europe since 2011.

After 5 years of successfully running my own freelance business, I pivoted into Software Engineering. Before making this shift, my freelance work was as a technical editor and translator alongside teaching English full-time at universities and business schools in the north of France.

My background is in the humanities, but with my technical editing work I completed a year-long Biostatistics course which allowed me to acquire an appreciation for clean data structures and databases. The writer in me appreciates data that tells a clear story.

In mid-May 2022, I graduated from General Assembly’s Software Engineering Immersive Course, where I learned JavaScript and Python fundamentals and built APIs using both the MERN Stack and Python Django framework. I completed 4 project sprints of 1 or 2 weeks each, which are the bootcamp projects included above.

By September 2022, Open Climate Fix, startup focused on decarbonizing the power grid, had hired me as a fully-remote Full Stack Intern, and I work for them now as a Full Stack Developer. I work on the main product, the Quartz Solar (formerly Nowcasting) App, a React and Typescript frontend that consumes a FastAPI and PostgreSQL backend. The team I'm on uses Agile methodology.

I am also the main developer on the team's UK Analysis Dashboard, a Streamlit App that displays PV forecast statistics for the OCF team. I’ve brought value to the team with my UI/UX design discussion and implementation skills.

Picking up on new things quickly, I’ve developed my data visualization skills learning the Pandas and Plotly Python libraries. I’ve also had fun discovering the OCF CI/CD infrastructure: Docker; Terraform Cloud; AWS.

why i love coding


Logic, aesthetics, and creativity come naturally to me, and coding is a space to express and explore all three at once. This way of making fuels my creativity while feeding my inner data-loving language learner with plenty of logic and syntax. There are many domains in which to forge a path. And at the end of the day, software engineering's a job that can provide the economic stability, teamwork, growth and intellectual challenge I sought in my career pivot.

resources

Here are a few resources I've found helpful as I start my coding journey.

Traversy Media has proved to be a great place for me to start. I connect easily to Brad Traversy's approach to coding and have found his tutorials from Python to Tailwind to be accessible and enjoyable to follow. And his Boston accent adds to his charm.

Discovering the Code Newbie podcast has been wonderful. Anytime I had a new concept in my bootcamp,I'd search their archives on the topic to have a listen. They also place a lot of emphasis on career-pivot coders, which I definitely connect to.

A List Apart was a recommendation from a friend who's been coding since the early 90's. This blog was super formative for him, and I've enjoyed reading their thoughtful articles both current and past. As I'm just transitioning to tech, this newsletter has provided some needed context.

There are few time management resources that have helped me more than Laura Vanderkamp's Time Tracking worksheet.
